About Me

Welcome to my website, which is currently under construction! My name is Dario and I am currently an MMATH Statistics student at the University of Waterloo. Previously, I was at the University of Victoria where I got my BSc in Mathematics and Statistics. Below I have some of my favourite projects, which include work in betting, forecasting, and Kolmogorov complexity. Outside of academics, I have been working as a painter (houses, not landscapes) for the past four years, though I have since retired. Recently, I came back from a backpacking trip through Asia where I visited Japan, Vietnam, Cambodia, and Thailand. I am also very influenced by the great books, articles, and movies I've consumed throughout the years, some of which I have listed below. My spare time consists of tennis matches, hiking, and conducting historical counterfactuals with friends (perferably over coffee or walking but never both).

Reading List

  • "Incerto" by Nassim Taleb: A series of books on uncertainty, chance, and risk that had a profound effect on my thinking processes.
  • "American Prometheus" by Kai Bird and Martin Sherwin: A deep dive into the science, history, and politics of the Manhattan Project that puts into perspective how significant science has become to the future of humanity.
  • "Fermat's Last Theorem" by Simon Singh: An amusing historical and mathematical account of FMT. A must-read for anyone with an ounce of interest in math.
